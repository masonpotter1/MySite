# Personal Site Content Strategy

## Content goals

The site should make Mason easy to understand, easy to remember, and easy to contact.

Professional content should be crisp and outcome-oriented. Personal content should be warmer, more visual, and more story-driven.

## Content sources

### Available now

- LinkedIn screenshot and public search result.
- User-provided travel notes.
- Existing website repository.
- UI mock-up direction.

### Pending

- Full resume.
- Real project names, links, screenshots, and metrics.
- GitHub URL.
- Preferred contact email.
- Full travel country list and photos.
- Final headshot or avatar.

## Voice and tone

### Do

- Sound confident, curious, and practical.
- Use concrete details.
- Let personality show through travel and interests.
- Keep recruiter content scannable.
- Explain business impact when possible.

### Avoid

- Generic claims like "passionate developer" without evidence.
- Overly formal resume language everywhere.
- Long paragraphs in recruiter-facing sections.
- Overstating private or confidential work.

## Photo strategy

Version 1 can use tasteful placeholders. Later, replace with:

- Professional headshot.
- Travel photos for Amsterdam, Paris, Italy, and other countries.
- Project screenshots.
- A subtle background image or abstract generated asset.

Photo treatment:

- Use dark overlays for readability.
- Keep images warm and slightly cinematic.
- Avoid cluttered backgrounds behind text.
